Realistic Event Volumes in Industrial SCADA
Typical modern SCADA setups handle polling cycles from 500 ms to 5 seconds per tag, but high-frequency ones (e.g., for vibration monitoring, fast pressure changes, or wind turbines) push toward 1 Hz (1 event/sec per tag) or even sub-second bursts. A large plant might have thousands of tags ‚Üí potentially hundreds to thousands of events per second during peaks, but most critical "meaningful" events (alarms, commands, changes) are far sparser‚Äîmaybe dozens to low hundreds per minute per site after filtering/normalization at the edge.
The PRD's edge gateway already batches + creates Merkle roots, so you're not anchoring every raw telemetry tick‚Äîjust the meaningful ones (e.g., TelemetryEvent summaries, AlarmEvent, BlueprintChangeEvent). This keeps volume manageable.
Current Ethereum L1 Anchoring Reality (Early 2026)

Gas prices are low (~0.035‚Äì0.164 Gwei range recently, with ETH around $3,000‚Äì$3,100).
Storing a 32-byte Merkle root via a simple SSTORE or calldata costs roughly 20,000‚Äì70,000 gas (depending on method), translating to pennies per anchor at current prices‚Äîeven during moderate congestion.
Batching every 5‚Äì15 minutes (or per shift) for a site means maybe 100‚Äì500 anchors per day across a fleet ‚Üí total daily cost in the low dollars range, even for multiple sites.

So for most realistic deployments, direct L1 anchoring is already affordable‚Äîespecially since the PRD emphasizes "batched (configurable cadence)" and focuses on critical state transitions, not firehose data.
If/When Volume Really Scales (High-Frequency + Multi-Site)
If you hit truly massive scale (e.g., federated multi-site with 10k+ events/minute needing frequent roots), or want sub-minute anchors, options to explore collectively:

Optimize Batch Cadence + Compression ‚Üí Stick to L1, but tune aggressively (e.g., anchor every 30‚Äì60 minutes, use tighter Merkle trees). This stays true to "Ethereum L1 as Ground Truth" without compromises.
Leverage Blobs (EIP-4844 / Proto-Danksharding) ‚Üí Since Dencun (2024), blobs are cheap temporary data (~128 KB each, target 3 per block, costs often under $0.01‚Äì$3 per blob depending on demand). You could post larger batched payloads or intermediate data as blobs, then anchor only the root on L1. This is still "Ethereum ecosystem" and preserves L1 as final arbiter‚Äîmany industrial IoT projects are eyeing this for high-volume data.
Hybrid with Modular DA Layers (e.g., Celestia) ‚Üí Celestia (modular DA) offers dramatically lower costs (~95% savings vs. Ethereum calldata in some cases) for posting data, with Ethereum still for settlement/identity if needed. This bends the "no sidechains/rollups for canonical truth" rule slightly (DA is off Ethereum), but keeps L1 sacred for proofs/identity.
